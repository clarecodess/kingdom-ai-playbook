Section 01 — The Transformation
Before This Playbook. After This Playbook.
Most business owners are using AI the way the world uses it — chasing efficiency, cutting costs, automating everything. This playbook changes the question you ask before every AI decision.
✗ Before: The World's Framework
Asking only "Can AI do this?"
Chasing efficiency at any human cost
No policy — you figure it out as you go
Treating AI output as trustworthy by default
Replacing human judgment silently
Eroding dignity without realizing it
✓ After: The Redemptive Standard
Asking "Can AI do this? Should it? How?"
Measuring success by human flourishing
A clear, biblical AI policy your team trusts
Verifying AI output against truth and values
Keeping humans accountable in decisions
Building businesses that reflect the image of God
"No one can serve two masters. For you will hate one and love the other; you will be devoted to one and despise the other. You cannot serve God and be enslaved to money."
Matthew 6:24 NLT — The core tension every founder must resolve
Section 02 — Why This Playbook Exists
Responsible AI Isn't Enough
The secular world built Responsible AI on two pillars: minimize harm and maximize profit. But you can't serve both God and money. Kingdom AI is a higher standard all Christians are called to — one built on Scripture, not just ethics checklists.
Responsible AI
Transparent, safe, ethical and profit-driven.
Kingdom AI
Transparent, safe, ethical and built on Scripture.
The Stakes
The secular world is setting norms for AI right now. The Church and Christian founders must bring a different voice.
Core Shift
The difference between Responsible AI and Kingdom AI is the difference between avoiding lawsuits and building the Kingdom. One minimizes harm. The other pursues human flourishing.
Section 03 — The Core Framework
The 5-Question Kingdom AI Filter
Before your team builds, buys, or deploys any AI tool, run it through these five questions. They aren't compliance checkboxes — they're conscience checkboxes.
Q1
Can AI do this?
Technical Feasibility
Q2
Should AI do this?
Ethical + Biblical Alignment
Q3
Who could be harmed?
Imago of God Test
Q4
Who holds the account?
Stewardship & Governance
Q5
Is this true?
Scripture-tested Discernment
Each question unpacked:
Q1 — Feasibility
Can AI actually do this well?
Don't deploy AI because it's trending. Verify that the tool performs reliably on this specific task. Many AI applications produce convincing but inaccurate output. Test before you trust.
Business application: Run a pilot before full deployment. Measure error rates.
Q2 — Ethics
Does this align with how God calls us to treat people?
If an AI application manipulates, deceives, dehumanizes, or exploits even subtly, it fails this question, regardless of ROI.
"Act justly, love mercy, and walk humbly with your God." — Micah 6:8 NLT
Q3 — Human Dignity
Does this honor the image of God in every person it touches?
Every customer, employee, vendor, and competitor bears the image of God. AI that treats people as data points fails this test even if it's efficient.
"So God created human beings in His own image." — Genesis 1:27 NLT
Q4 — Accountability
Who is responsible when this AI causes harm?
Stewardship means you own the outcomes. "The AI decided" is not a biblical response to harm caused. Build accountability before you deploy.
"Yes, each of us will give a personal account to God." — Romans 14:12 NLT
Q5 — Truth
Is what AI produces tested against Scripture?
AI generates the most probable words, not necessarily truthful ones. Plausible is not the same as true.
"Make them holy by Your truth, teach them Your word, which is truth." — John 17:17 NLT

Section 04 — Biblical Foundation
7 Principles at a Glance
These are the convictions that can shape every AI decision in a Kingdom business. Use them as gut checks, not compliance checkboxes.
Principle 01
Human Dignity
Every human bears God's image in ways AI cannot replicate. Protect irreducible human worth in every AI application.
Genesis 1:26–27
Principle 02
Purpose & Meaning
Build foundational skills first. AI should accelerate God-given callings. Never replace the formation process.
Proverbs 22:29
Principle 03
Ethical Boundaries
Just because we can doesn't mean we should. Set firm limits especially where human lives are involved.
Micah 6:8
Principle 04
Stewardship
We are stewards of AI, not its advocates. Measure success by human flourishing not just efficiency gains.
1 Corinthians 10:31
Principle 05
Community & Relationality
AI that replaces human presence corrodes trust and community. Build connections AI cannot replicate.
John 13:34
Principle 06
Responsibility

Philippians 1:9–10
Principle 07
Guard Against Deception

John 17:17

Marketing · 01
AI-Generated Personalization That Crosses Into Manipulation
What businesses are doing
Using AI to analyze buyer psychology and serve hyper-personalized urgency messaging — countdown timers, scarcity signals, and FOMO copy generated at scale to maximize conversions.
What's wrong biblically
This treats customers as targets, not image-bearers. When AI exploits fear or urgency to override rational decision-making, it crosses from persuasion into manipulation.
Principle 03 — Micah 6:8: "Act justly, love mercy"
What to do instead
Use AI to personalize relevance without engineering psychological pressure. Ask: "Would this message honor the customer if they knew how it was crafted?"
Marketing · 02
AI-Written Content Presented as Human Thought Leadership
What businesses are doing
Executives publish AI-drafted LinkedIn articles and newsletters as personal insights without disclosure. Entire personal brands are now synthetic.
What's wrong biblically
Presenting AI output as your own thought is deception — a violation of Principle 06 and 07. Silent AI ghostwriting breaks the covenant of honesty with your audience.
Principle 06 — "Trust is gained in drops and lost in buckets"
What to do instead
Use AI to draft and structure — but own the voice, convictions, and final words. "AI-assisted" is honest. Your audience will sense the difference.
Marketing · 03
AI Customer Segmentation That Creates Hidden Discrimination
What businesses are doing
Using AI models trained on historical data, unknowingly encoding past biases — excluding lower-income communities from opportunities.
What's wrong biblically
"Bias at scale" is still injustice, regardless of whether a human made the decision. We are called to serve the common good and watch for who gets left behind.
Principle 04 — Stewardship
What to do instead
Audit segmentation models for demographic disparities. Build equity reviews into quarterly AI audits.
Marketing · 04
AI-Generated Testimonials and Synthetic Social Proof
What businesses are doing
Using AI to generate fake reviews, synthetic testimonial videos using deepfake technology, or AI images of "satisfied customers."
What's wrong biblically
Fabricating social proof is bearing false witness. Principle 07: God's people are anchored in truth, not manufacturing it.
Principle 07 — John 14:6: Truth is non-negotiable
What to do instead
Build genuine review systems. Use AI to prompt customers for feedback and organize real testimonials. Real trust is a sustainable competitive advantage.
Marketing · 05
Replacing Creative Teams Entirely With AI-Generated Content
What businesses are doing
Eliminating copywriters, graphic designers, and creative strategists — replacing them with AI pipelines that produce "good enough" content at a fraction of the cost.
What's wrong biblically
Human creativity reflects the image of God. It's a spiritual activity, not just an economic function. Eliminating creative roles violates Principle 01 and 02.
Principle 01 — Genesis 1:27: Human creativity reflects God's creative nature
What to do instead
Redesign creative roles rather than eliminate them. AI handles the 80%. Humans own the 20% that matters most.
Customer Support · 01
AI Chatbots Handling Emotional Crisis Without Human Escalation
What businesses are doing
Deploying AI chatbots as the primary support channel in healthcare and financial services, with no human escalation pathway for customers in distress.
What's wrong biblically
Routing hurting people to algorithms fails the most basic call of Christian business: to love our neighbor as ourselves.
Principle 05 — John 13:34: "Love one another as I have loved you"
What to do instead
Define mandatory human escalation triggers: any mention of distress, loss, financial crisis, or health emergency. Build escalation as a core feature, not an afterthought.
Customer Support · 02
AI Pretending to Be a Named Human Agent
What businesses are doing
Giving AI chatbots human names ("Hi, I'm Sarah!") and personas, deliberately concealing that the customer is speaking to an AI.
What's wrong biblically
This is straightforward deception — a direct violation of Principle 07. It corrodes the trust that makes business relationships possible.
Principle 07 — Truth is non-negotiable
What to do instead
Be transparent. Name your AI what it is: "Hi, I'm Aria — your AI assistant." Research shows customers respect honesty more than they resist AI.
Customer Support · 03
AI Voice Cloning for Customer Calls
What businesses are doing
Using AI voice-cloning to create synthetic versions of support staff, deployed at scale without the employee's full knowledge.
What's wrong biblically
This violates both the employee (used without consent) and the customer (deceived about human presence). Principle 01 applies to employees too.
Principle 01 — The image of God applies to your employees too
What to do instead
Never clone an employee's voice without explicit ongoing consent and full customer disclosure. People are not content to be cloned.
Customer Support · 04
AI Sentiment Analysis Used to Prioritize "Valuable" Customers
What businesses are doing
Using revenue scoring to route tickets — faster service to high-value customers while others wait for templated AI responses.
What's wrong biblically
James 2:1–4 warns against showing favoritism. Algorithmic favoritism is the modern version of dishonoring the poor. Every customer bears the image of God.
Principle 04 — James 2:1–4: No favoritism. Every person has equal dignity.
What to do instead
Route by urgency and complexity of need — not revenue tier. Use AI to make standard support excellent for everyone.
Customer Support · 05
AI Collecting Sensitive Data Under the Guise of "Better Service"
What businesses are doing
AI support systems harvest emotional and behavioral data from conversations and feed them into marketing engines without the customer's knowledge.
What's wrong biblically
Using disclosed vulnerabilities for commercial exploitation is a profound betrayal of trust and a violation of loving our neighbor.
Principle 06 — "Protect human privacy and dignity in all data practices"
What to do instead
Create a firm policy: data shared in support contexts stays in support contexts. Earn trust, don't mine it.
Hiring · 01
AI Resume Screening That Buries Qualified Candidates
What businesses are doing
Using AI trained on historical hiring data — replicating whoever was hired in the past. Amazon scrapped its tool after it penalized resumes with the word "women's."
What's wrong biblically
Automating biased past decisions scales injustice. God shows no partiality (Acts 10:34). AI that screens out certain groups buries dignity.
Principle 04 — Acts 10:34: "God shows no partiality"
What to do instead
Audit hiring AI annually for demographic disparities. No candidate should be rejected without human eyes on their application.
Hiring · 02
AI Video Interviews That Assess Personality Without Consent
What businesses are doing
Using AI to score facial microexpressions, speech patterns, and tone in video interviews without candidates' knowledge. Scientific validity is hotly disputed.
What's wrong biblically
Reducing a human being to facial microexpressions treats people as data sources, not image-bearers. Principle 01 and 07 both apply.
Principle 01 + 07 — Dignity and Truth must both be honored
What to do instead
Disclose exactly what AI is assessing. Give candidates the ability to opt out. Use AI for logistics, not personality scoring.
Hiring · 03
Eliminating Roles With AI Without Transition Support
What businesses are doing
Eliminating entry-level and mid-level positions as AI handles the work, with no transition plan, no retraining investment, and minimal notice.
What's wrong biblically
"When the role becomes economically unviable, we're not saying the person is economically unviable." Treating people as interchangeable units is a Principle 02 failure.
Principle 02 — Ephesians 2:10: People are God's handiwork, not headcount
What to do instead
Plan for role transitions before deploying AI at scale. Create retraining pathways funded by efficiency gains. Give affected employees first access to new roles.
Hiring · 04
AI Reference Checks That Score Based on Tone Analysis
What businesses are doing
Using AI to score the sentiment and enthusiasm of references, flagging "lukewarm" ones automatically without the candidate's knowledge.
What's wrong biblically
Reduces human testimony to algorithmic sentiment scoring. Cultural communication styles vary; AI-scored references risk encoding cultural bias into hiring decisions.
Principle 03 + 07 — Set boundaries on AI; test outputs for truth
What to do instead
Use AI for reference logistics — not for scoring relational content. Algorithms can't replace discernment.
Hiring · 05
Using AI to Scrape Candidate Data Without Permission
What businesses are doing
Using AI tools that scrape social media, court records, and consumer databases to build candidate profiles far beyond what was shared with the company.
What's wrong biblically
This violates the dignity and privacy of people who haven't consented. It treats a person's whole life as data to be mined — not a human to be respected.
Principle 03 — "Protect human privacy and dignity in all data practices"
What to do instead
Ask: "Would this candidate consent to this if we told them?" If the answer is no, don't do it.
Operations · 01
AI Surveillance of Remote Employees
What businesses are doing
Tracking keystrokes, mouse movements, screenshots every 5 minutes, webcam eye-tracking, and Slack sentiment — generating "productivity scores" for each employee.
What's wrong biblically
Pervasive surveillance treats employees as suspects, not trusted image-bearers. It corrodes relational trust that makes teams flourish.
Principle 05 — Community corrodes when presence becomes surveillance
What to do instead
Disclose every monitoring capability fully. Use AI to track outcomes and deliverables, not moment-to-moment activity. Manage by results, not keystrokes.
Operations · 02
AI Used for Vendor Selection Without Human Due Diligence
What businesses are doing
Outsourcing vendor evaluation entirely to AI platforms, making selection decisions algorithmically without reviewing the ethical record of vendors.
What's wrong biblically
Stewardship requires active wisdom, not passive algorithmic deference. "The AI chose them" is not a stewardship defense.
Principle 04 + 06 — Stewards own outcomes. Algorithms don't.
What to do instead
Use AI to gather and organize vendor data — never to make final vendor decisions autonomously. Require human sign-off on all vendor selections.
Operations · 03
AI-Automated Pricing That Exploits Vulnerable Customers
What businesses are doing
Dynamic pricing AI raises prices in real-time based on demand signals — surge pricing during emergencies, exploiting travelers stranded by cancellations.
What's wrong biblically
Proverbs 11:26 warns against exploiting scarcity for maximum extraction. Dynamic pricing that exploits maximum vulnerability violates loving our neighbor.
Principle 04 — Proverbs 11:26: Don't exploit need. Serve it.
What to do instead
Build ethical guardrails: price caps during emergencies, policies against exploiting distress signals, and transparency about how pricing varies.
Operations · 04
Using a Vendor Whose AI Ethics Contradict Your Values
What businesses are doing
Most businesses don't investigate the ethical record of AI platforms they use, selecting based on capability and cost alone.
What's wrong biblically
The companies that create AI tools are not neutral. When you pay a vendor, you fund their practices. Principle 03: "Does using this platform financially support harmful activities?"
Principle 03 — The Vendor Ethics Question demands an answer
What to do instead
Create a "vendor values scorecard" aligned with your principles. Make the decision prayerfully and explicitly, not by default.
Operations · 05
AI Decisions With No Documented Accountability Owner
What businesses are doing
Deploying AI tools without assigning a human owner accountable for outputs and ethical implications. "The system did it" becomes the default when harm occurs.
What's wrong biblically
Romans 14:12 is direct: accountability is not optional for stewards. Authority without accountability is irresponsible stewardship.
Principle 06 — Romans 14:12: Every AI decision needs a human owner
What to do instead
The rule is: no AI in production without a named human accountable for its behavior. Build an AI Governance Register updated quarterly.
Section 06 — Warning Signs
Red Flags Reference
Screenshot this. Post it in your Slack. Share it in your next leadership meeting. These are the warning signs that your AI use has drifted from Kingdom to Responsible — or worse.
🔴
No Human Override
Any AI system making decisions about people with no clear human override mechanism. If no one can say "stop" — it's a governance failure.
🔴
"The AI Decided"
Any organization where "the algorithm decided" is an acceptable final answer when a human is harmed. Algorithms don't bear moral responsibility — you do.
🔴
No Disclosure Policy
AI-generated content, chatbots, or communications sent without any disclosure policy. Silence is deception by omission.
🔴
Speed Over Accuracy
Deploying AI quickly before its accuracy and safety have been validated. "Move fast and break things" is a Babel mindset, not a Kingdom one.
🔴
AI in Care Roles
Any chatbot positioned as a counselor or emotional support tool without immediate human escalation pathways built in.
🔴
Unchecked Vendor Use
Using AI platforms whose content policies, data practices, or leadership values you haven't reviewed. Ignorance is not innocence.
🔴
Efficiency Over Dignity
Any decision where saving money was the primary justification for removing human involvement from a process that affects people's dignity or livelihood.
🔴
No Audit Trail
AI tools operating with no logging, no review process, and no ability to understand what decisions they made and why. If you can't audit it, you can't own it.
🔴
Scripture Untested
Any AI-generated content touching theology or spiritual guidance published without human theological review. AI produces plausible — not necessarily true.
🔴
Silent Surveillance
Monitoring employees, customers, or vendors using AI tools whose scope has never been disclosed to them.

[Company Name] is committed to using artificial intelligence in ways that honor human dignity, pursue truth, serve our community, and reflect our biblical values. We recognize that AI is a powerful tool — and that tools require wisdom, not just skill, to use well. We are responsible stewards of AI.

Customize: Insert your company name and add 1–2 sentences about what your business values specifically.
2. What AI We Use and Why
We currently use the following AI tools in our operations:

[Tool Name] — [Department] — [Purpose]
e.g., ChatGPT — Marketing — First-draft copy generation (reviewed and approved by human team before publication)

Customize: List every AI tool your team uses. This forces clarity and creates accountability.
3. What AI May Not Do (Non-Negotiable Limits)
The following uses of AI are permanently off-limits at [Company Name], regardless of efficiency gains:

• AI may not interact with customers in emotional distress without immediate human escalation
• AI may not make final hiring or firing decisions
• AI may not generate or publish theological content without human pastoral review
• AI may not be presented as a human to customers or partners
• AI may not collect or use personal data beyond what customers have explicitly consented to
• AI may not monitor employee activity without full disclosure and employee acknowledgment

Customize: Add your organization-specific limits based on your industry and principles.
4. Disclosure Standards
We are committed to transparency about our use of AI:

• All AI-assisted content will be disclosed using: [e.g., "Drafted with AI assistance, reviewed and approved by our team"]
• Customer-facing AI tools will be clearly identified as AI — not given human names or personas
• Employees will be informed of all AI monitoring tools used in their work environment

Customize: Set your specific disclosure language for each channel.
5. Accountability Structure
AI Policy Owner: [Name, Title] — Overall accountability for AI governance
Review Cadence: Quarterly AI audit covering tool performance, ethical incidents, and policy compliance
Escalation Path: Any employee who identifies an AI-related ethical concern reports to [Name/Role] within [timeframe]
Override Authority: [Name/Role] has authority to pause or discontinue any AI tool pending review

Customize: Name real people. Vague accountability is no accountability.
6. Our Biblical Foundation
This policy is grounded in the belief that every human being bears the image of God (Genesis 1:27), that we are called to act justly, love mercy, and walk humbly (Micah 6:8), and that we are stewards — not owners — of the resources, tools, and people entrusted to us (1 Corinthians 10:31). We will use AI to pursue the Kingdom, not merely profit.
Section 08 — Decision Tools
The Kingdom AI Decision Checklist
Use this before deploying any new AI tool or process. Check each item and only proceed if you can answer honestly.
Feasibility & Purpose
We have tested this AI tool for accuracy in our specific use case — not just in demos.
We have identified a clear, specific purpose this AI serves — not just deployed it because it's available.
We have confirmed this AI serves a genuine human need, not just an efficiency metric.
Human Dignity
We have identified every person this AI touches and considered their dignity.
We have determined which interactions must remain human-to-human.
We have built mandatory human escalation triggers for high-emotion or high-stakes interactions.
Ethics & Boundaries
We have reviewed the AI vendor's content policies, data practices, and ethical record.
We have documented clear limits for what this AI may and may not do in our organization.
We have considered whether this AI could be used to manipulate, deceive, or exploit — and guarded against it.
Transparency & Disclosure
We have a disclosure standard — customers/employees know when they're interacting with AI.
AI-generated content produced by this tool will be labeled or attributed appropriately.
We have communicated this AI's role internally so our team understands their accountability.
Accountability & Governance
A named human is accountable for this AI tool's outputs, errors, and ethical behavior.
We have a review process scheduled (at minimum quarterly) to audit this tool.
There is a clear process for pausing or removing this AI tool if it causes harm.
We have documented this AI use case in our AI Policy and shared it with relevant stakeholders.
Final Question
If every customer, employee, and community member affected by this AI knew exactly how it worked and why we deployed it — would we be proud of that conversation? If not: redesign before you deploy.
Section 09 — Bonus Materials
Bonus: Navigating Tensions
These seven principles don't always agree with each other. When they collide, here's how to navigate the tension without abandoning either side.
Privacy vs. Transparency
Be transparent externally about what AI categories you use; be discreet internally about individual-level data.
⚠️ Never let "transparency" become a cover for publishing individual employee data without consent.
Stewardship vs. Dignity
Redemptive stewardship invests efficiency gains back into people — not just optimizing cost structure.
✓ Rule: Use AI efficiency gains to fund human flourishing — retraining, new roles, community investment.
Speed vs. Discernment
Build a lean 72-hour discernment sprint for every new AI tool — run the 5 Questions, assign an owner, set review dates.
✓ Rule: Fast deployment + slow review is not stewardship. Build the process in advance.
Community vs. Accessibility
Use AI to scale access; use humans to deliver presence. These are complementary when deployed with intention.
✓ Rule: AI scales the what. Humans sustain the who.
Bonus: The Vendor Ethics Evaluation
Before signing any AI vendor contract, answer these five questions:
What content does this vendor's AI model generate — and does it generate content that contradicts our values or Scripture?
How was this model's training data sourced — and was it sourced ethically with appropriate permissions?
What are the vendor's stated values — and are they compatible with ours, or do they actively contradict them?
Does using this vendor financially support activities or initiatives we would not otherwise support?
If our customers and community knew we used this vendor — would we be able to explain the decision with confidence and conviction?
